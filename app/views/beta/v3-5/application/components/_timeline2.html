  2. Step 2: Next: Submit applicaiton

    Continue to edit and submit your application

  3. Step 3: Certifier to review application

    Once submited your certifier can review the certificate.

  4. Step 4: Import notice created on TRACES

    If the application is approved an import notice will be created automatically on TRACES.

  5. Step 5: Send approved application to hauler

    You will recive a QR code that the hauler can use at boarder control. You no londer need print a paper certificate to go with your shipment.

  6. Step 6: Link CHED with import notice.

    You, or the person/organisation who is responsible for the load (RFL) – this can typically be your customer, haulier or agent – can now prepare a Common Health Entry Document (CHED) on TRACES. This is a required step before the goods can enter the EU.

    To reduce the amount of manual data entry required, you or your RFL can link the CHED with the import notice, using the Certificate reference or IMSOC reference from above.
