How paperless applications work

Entering details of your consignment

The information that you enter about your consignment – for example, species, quantity and method of transport – will be validated against the EU’s Trade Control and Expert System (TRACES). This ensures the data is consistent with the requirements of the country you are sending your consignment to.

Certifier checks

Once you’ve entered information about your consignment, your certifier can use the online service to:

  • review the information you have entered
  • enter information for any sections you skipped
  • cancel and replace the application if there are any changes to the consignment

Creating a common health entry document (CHED)

Once your certifier has sent the application to TRACES for validation, the importer will be able to create a CHED

When the consignment arrives at the border control post

The driver responsible for your consignment will be able to download a unique QR code to show at the border control post (BCP).

You will be able to check whether your BCP accepts paperless applications before you submit your application. If they cannot accept a paperless application, you will need to submit a paper EHC instead.
